Courses After 10th

Explore different streams, vocational courses, and integrated programs available after 10th grade.

Science Stream (PCM)

The Science stream with PCM is ideal for students interested in technical fields like engineering, architecture, or pure sciences. This stream requires strong analytical and problem-solving skills.

PCM Subjects

Subjects:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Mathematics

Physics (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Physics is a fundamental subject that deals with the laws of nature and the properties of matter and energy.

Class 11 Physics Syllabus:

  • Physical World and Measurement: Units, dimensions, errors, and the basics of physical quantities.
  • Kinematics: Motion in a straight line, relative motion, velocity, and acceleration.
  • Laws of Motion: Newton’s laws, momentum, friction, and equilibrium.
  • Work, Energy, and Power: Concepts of work, energy, and power, kinetic and potential energy.
  • System of Particles and Rotational Motion: Rigid bodies, rotational dynamics, and moment of inertia.
  • Gravitation: Universal law of gravitation, planetary motion, and satellites.
  • Properties of Bulk Matter: Elasticity, pressure, surface tension, and viscosity.
  • Thermodynamics: Laws of thermodynamics, heat transfer, and kinetic theory of gases.
  • Oscillations and Waves: Simple harmonic motion, wave motion, and sound waves.

Class 12 Physics Syllabus:

  • Electrostatics: Electric charges, electric fields, potential, and capacitance.
  • Current Electricity: Ohm’s law, Kirchhoff’s rules, and electrical circuits.
  • Magnetic Effects of Current & Magnetism: Biot-Savart law, Ampere’s law, and electromagnetic induction.
  • Electromagnetic Waves: Nature of electromagnetic waves and the electromagnetic spectrum.
  • Optics: Ray optics, wave optics, reflection, refraction, and interference.
  • Dual Nature of Matter and Radiation: Photoelectric effect and quantum mechanics.
  • Atoms and Nuclei: Atomic models, nuclear reactions, and radioactivity.
  • Electronic Devices: Semiconductors, diodes, and transistors.

Recommended Reference Books:

  • NCERT Physics (Class 11 & 12)
  • Concepts of Physics by H.C. Verma (Volume 1 & 2)
  • Problems in General Physics by I.E. Irodov

Chemistry (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Chemistry deals with the composition, properties, and structure of substances and the changes they undergo.

Class 11 Chemistry Syllabus:

  • Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry: Atomic mass, mole concept, and stoichiometry.
  • Structure of Atom: Quantum numbers, electronic configuration, and atomic models.
  • Chemical Bonding and Molecular Structure: Covalent, ionic bonding, and VSEPR theory.
  • States of Matter: Gases, liquids, and intermolecular forces.
  • Thermodynamics: First and second laws of thermodynamics, enthalpy, and entropy.
  • Equilibrium: Chemical equilibrium, acids, bases, and ionic equilibrium.
  • Redox Reactions: Oxidation, reduction, and balancing redox reactions.
  • Organic Chemistry – Some Basic Principles and Techniques: Hybridization, classification of organic compounds, and IUPAC nomenclature.

Class 12 Chemistry Syllabus:

  • Solid State: Crystalline and amorphous solids, unit cells.
  • Solutions: Concentration, colligative properties, and Raoult’s law.
  • Electrochemistry: Electrolytes, galvanic cells, and Nernst equation.
  • Chemical Kinetics: Rate of reaction, order, and molecularity.
  • Surface Chemistry: Adsorption, catalysis, colloids, and emulsions.
  • p-Block Elements: Group 15 to 18 elements and their properties.
  • d- and f-Block Elements: Transition elements, lanthanides, and actinides.
  • Coordination Compounds: Nomenclature, bonding, and importance.
  • Haloalkanes and Haloarenes: Reactions, preparation, and uses.

Recommended Reference Books:

  • NCERT Chemistry (Class 11 & 12)
  • Organic Chemistry by Morrison and Boyd
  • Physical Chemistry by P.W. Atkins

Mathematics (Class 11 & 12 Syllabus):

Mathematics is the study of numbers, quantities, shapes, and patterns and their relationships.

Class 11 Mathematics Syllabus:

  • Sets, Relations, and Functions: Basic set theory, types of relations, functions, and binary operations.
  • Trigonometry: Functions, equations, and identities related to trigonometry.
  • Complex Numbers and Quadratic Equations: Basic algebraic operations with complex numbers and solving quadratic equations.
  • Permutations and Combinations: Fundamental principles of counting and factorial notation.
  • Binomial Theorem: Expansion using Pascal’s triangle and application of binomial theorem.
  • Sequences and Series: Arithmetic progression (AP) and geometric progression (GP).
  • Straight Lines and Conic Sections: Equations and properties of lines, circles, parabolas, ellipses, and hyperbolas.
  • Limits and Derivatives: Introduction to limits, continuity, and basic differentiation.

Class 12 Mathematics Syllabus:

  • Matrices and Determinants: Matrix operations, properties, and solving systems of linear equations.
  • Calculus: Differentiation and integration with practical applications.
  • Vectors and 3D Geometry: Vector operations, planes, and lines in three-dimensional space.
  • Probability: Basic probability concepts, Bayes' theorem, and random variables.
  • Linear Programming: Formulating and solving linear optimization problems.

Recommended Reference Books:

  • NCERT Mathematics (Class 11 & 12)
  • Mathematics for Class 11 & 12 by R.D. Sharma
  • Problems in Calculus of One Variable by I.A. Maron

Future Paths:

  • Engineering (B.Tech/B.E.): Specializations in Computer Science, Mechanical, Civil, Electrical, etc.
  • Architecture (B.Arch): Designing buildings and structures.
  • Pure Sciences (B.Sc): Fields like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, etc.
  • Merchant Navy: Training to become a ship officer.

Preparation Tips:

Strong focus on Mathematics and Physics. Regular practice of numerical problems and concepts. Enroll in coaching classes for engineering entrance exams (like JEE).

Science Stream (PCB)

The PCB combination is suited for students aiming for careers in medicine, biology-related fields, or research.

Future Paths:

  • Medicine (MBBS): Becoming a doctor.
  • Dental (BDS): Specialization in dental care.
  • Biotechnology: A field merging biology and technology.
  • Pharmacy (B.Pharm): Study of medicines and drugs.
  • Nursing: Providing healthcare and support.

Subjects:

  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Biology
  • Optional Subject (Mathematics/Computer Science/Physical Education, etc.)

Preparation Tips:

Focus on Biology and Chemistry. Practice diagrams and theoretical concepts. Prepare for medical entrance exams (like NEET).

Commerce Stream

Commerce is ideal for students interested in business, finance, and economics.

Future Paths:

  • Chartered Accountancy (CA): Professional accounting qualification.
  • Company Secretary (CS): Legal aspects of corporate governance.
  • Economics (B.A./B.Sc. Economics): Study of economic theories and applications.
  • Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA): Management and business studies.
  • Actuarial Science: Risk management and insurance.

Subjects:

  • Accountancy
  • Business Studies
  • Economics
  • Optional Subject (Mathematics/Informatics Practices/Entrepreneurship, etc.)

Preparation Tips:

Focus on understanding financial concepts and economic theories. Practice accounting problems and case studies. Join coaching classes for professional courses like CA or CS.

Arts/Humanities Stream

The Arts stream offers a broad range of subjects, suitable for students interested in social sciences, law, literature, and creative fields.

Future Paths:

  • Law (LLB): Legal studies and practice.
  • Journalism and Mass Communication: Media and communication careers.
  • Fine Arts (BFA): Creative arts like painting, sculpture, etc.
  • Design (Fashion, Interior, Graphic): Creative and design fields.
  • Social Work (BSW): Community service and social work.

Subjects:

  • History
  • Geography
  • Political Science
  • Sociology/Psychology/English Literature (optional subjects)

Preparation Tips:

Read extensively to improve understanding of social concepts. Engage in discussions and debates to enhance analytical skills. Consider internships in media, NGOs, or creative fields.

Vocational Courses

Vocational courses provide practical skills and training in specific trades or professions.

Future Paths:

  • Polytechnic (Engineering Diploma): Technical education leading to junior engineer roles.
  • Fashion Designing: Creative roles in fashion industry.
  • Interior Designing: Designing indoor spaces.
  • Animation and Multimedia: Careers in animation, gaming, and digital media.
  • Web Designing: Creating and managing websites.

Subjects:

  • Subjects vary based on the course selected.
  • Typically include practical and theoretical aspects of the chosen trade.

Preparation Tips:

Focus on hands-on training and practical application of skills. Consider internships or apprenticeships for real-world experience. Stay updated with industry trends and technologies.

Integrated Courses

Integrated courses offer combined undergraduate and postgraduate education, providing a streamlined educational path.

Future Paths:

  • B.Tech + M.Tech: Integrated engineering education.
  • B.Sc + M.Sc: Combined science degrees.
  • BA + LLB: Integrated law education.
  • Integrated MBA: Management studies.

Subjects:

  • Varies based on the integrated course selected.
  • Typically includes a combination of undergraduate and graduate-level subjects.

Preparation Tips:

Focus on long-term career goals. Be prepared for intensive study periods. Engage in internships or industry projects to enhance practical knowledge.
